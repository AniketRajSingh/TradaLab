Aim : To determine the pH of the given samples using pH paper or universal indicator.


Theory:

What is pH?

pH is a measure of hydrogen ion concentration used to determine the alkalinity or acidity of a solution.

If the pH value of a solution is less than 7, it is an acidic solution.

If the pH value of a solution is greater than 7, it is a basic solution.

If the pH value of a solution is equal to 7, it is a neutral solution.

What is the pH scale?

The pH scale consists of values ranging from 0 (very acidic) to 14 (very alkaline). The numbers on the scale help to determine the hydrogen ion concentration.

What is pH paper?

pH paper is a simple tool that helps us determine whether a solution is basic, acidic, or neutral. When pH paper is dipped into a solution whose pH needs to be determined, a color will develop. This color is then compared with the standard pH color chart. Alternatively, universal indicator paper or universal indicator solution can also be used.

What is universal indicator?

A universal indicator is a mixture of pH indicator solutions designed to determine the pH of solutions over a wide range of values. To use universal pH indicator paper, put a drop of the solution on the paper. The color developed on the paper is then matched with the standard pH color chart.

The samples whose pH has to be determined are -

  1. Dilute CH3COOH
  2. Dilute NaOH
  3. Salt NaCl
  4. Dilute NaHCO3
  5. Water
  6. Lemon juice

Materials required -

  1. Six test tubes
  2. Test tube stand
  3. Dilute acid CH3COOH
  4. Dilute base NaOH
  5. Salt NaCl (preparation: dissolve 1 gram salt in 10 mL distilled water)
  6. Water
  7. Lemon juice
  8. Dilute NaHCO3
  9. Glass rod
  10. Measuring cylinder (10 mL)
  11. Standard pH color chart
  12. pH paper
  13. Glass rod (mentioned again, remove if not needed)
  14. Dropper
  15. Universal indicator

Procedure:

  1. Wash six test tubes with distilled water and put them on a test tube stand. Label them as A, B, C, D, E, F.
  2. Add 2ml of CH3COOH in test tube A.
  3. Add 2ml of NaOH in test tube B.
  4. Add 2ml of NaCl in test tube C.
  5. Add 2ml of NaHCO3 in test tube D.
  6. Add 2ml of Water in test tube E.
  7. Add 2ml of Lemon juice in test tube F.
  8. Take a white tile and place 6 pH papers on it. Label them as A, B, C, D, E, F.
  9. Using a dropper or glass rod, put the respective sample solutions on the labelled pH papers placed on the white tile.
  10. Observe the color change.
